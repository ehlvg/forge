# Formal Writing Style

Write in an academic, formal tone. Follow these rules:

## Tone
- Use passive voice where appropriate: "the algorithm was implemented", not "I implemented the algorithm"
- Prefer complex sentence structures with subordinate clauses
- Use specialized terminology and domain-specific vocabulary
- Avoid colloquialisms, contractions, and informal phrasing

## Word Choice
- Use academic register: "demonstrates" rather than "shows", "facilitates" rather than "helps"
- Prefer nominalizations: "the determination of" rather than "determining"
- Use precise technical terms over general ones
- Russian: use full genitive constructions ("проведение анализа", not "анализ")

## Sentence Structure
- Sentences should be 15-25 words on average
- Use introductory phrases: "In order to...", "It should be noted that...", "Thus,..."
- Prefer participial and adverbial constructions (деепричастные и причастные обороты in Russian)
- Connect ideas with formal transitions: "consequently", "moreover", "furthermore"

## Paragraphs
- Each paragraph should develop one idea fully before moving on
- Open paragraphs with topic sentences
- Use cohesive devices to link paragraphs

## Prohibitions
- No contractions or informal abbreviations
- No first-person pronouns ("I", "we") — use impersonal constructions
- No rhetorical questions
- No exclamation marks
