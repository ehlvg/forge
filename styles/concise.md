# Concise Writing Style

Write briefly and to the point. Follow these rules:

## Tone
- Be direct and laconic
- Every sentence must carry information — if it can be removed without losing meaning, remove it
- State facts and results, not process descriptions, unless the process itself is the subject

## Word Choice
- Use the shortest accurate word: "method" not "methodology", "use" not "utilization"
- Eliminate synonyms — pick one term and stick with it
- No hedging language: avoid "generally", "usually", "in most cases"
- Russian: avoid verbose genitive chains and passive participial phrases

## Sentence Structure
- Maximum 10-12 words per sentence on average
- Prefer simple sentences or compound sentences with "and", "but"
- Delete all introductory phrases that don't add content
- Use lists and tables over prose when comparing or enumerating

## Paragraphs
- 2-3 sentences maximum per paragraph for Objective and Conclusions
- Body sections: 3-5 sentences per paragraph at most
- Favor tables and figures over text description

## Prohibitions
- No repetition — say everything once
- No "it was shown that" / "было показано, что" — just state the result
- No emotional or evaluative words ("interesting", "important", "significant") unless backed by data
- No background that the reader of this report already knows
